Our story

It started with one question: why is medicine the thing families skip?

Project MedBridge was founded by a student who kept hearing the same story from family and community connections in India: people weren't dying from rare diseases. They were getting sicker from common, treatable conditions — diabetes, hypertension, asthma, infections — because a month of medication cost more than a family could spare.

The gap felt absurd. A few dollars in America is a month of insulin somewhere else. So we built a bridge: we fundraise here, partner with NGOs already embedded in these communities, and make every donation traceable to a real person's treatment.

What we stand on

Three commitments guide every campaign we run.

🤝

Local partners, local knowledge

We don't parachute in. Our partner NGOs already live and work in the communities they serve. They identify patients, verify needs, purchase medicine locally, and deliver it — we fund the gap.

🔍

Radical transparency

Every patient campaign shows exactly how much has been raised and how much is needed. When a campaign completes, we post confirmation from the NGO that the medicine was delivered.

🛡️

Dignity first

Every story on our site is shared with the patient's consent through our partner NGO. We share first names only, and patients can ask for their story to be removed at any time.

Questions donors ask us

How do I know the money actually reaches patients?

Each campaign is tied to a specific patient identified by a partner NGO. When their funding goal is met, we transfer the funds and the NGO purchases and delivers the medicine locally, then sends us confirmation. We mark the campaign complete on the site.

Are these real people? Are the stories verified?

Yes. Patients are identified and verified by our partner NGOs, who work in these communities every day. Stories are shared with consent, using first names only.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

We are in the process of establishing our nonprofit status. Check back here — once confirmed, donations will be tax-deductible and we'll provide receipts.

What happens if a campaign raises more than the goal?

Extra funds roll into the general Medicine Fund, which supports the most urgent campaigns and restocks partner NGO community pharmacies.

Can I help without donating money?

Absolutely. Sharing a patient's story is one of the most effective things you can do — most campaigns get funded because someone shared them. You can also reach out about volunteering at hello@projectmedbridge.org.
